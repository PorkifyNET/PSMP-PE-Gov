Imperial Laws & Regulations

This page provides consolidated access to public laws and regulations in force across the Piggian Empire. For historical authenticity: the legally authoritative publication is the Imperial Gazette.

Legal notice: This portal is provided for public information. In case of discrepancies, the text as published in the Imperial Gazette (official register) prevails.

Language: The primary publication language is Imperial German. Dutch and English versions, where shown, are provided as non-binding public translations.

Hierarchy of Law

Sources of Imperial Law

The legal order of the Empire is organized by authority and scope. Lower instruments may not conflict with higher instruments.

  1. Imperial Basic Charter (constitutional law)
  2. Imperial Acts (statutes passed by the Imperial Parliament)
  3. Imperial Decrees (executive instruments issued under enabling authority)
  4. Ministerial Regulations (technical rules issued by ministries)
  5. Provincial Ordinances (local rules, consistent with imperial law)
Citation formats used on this portal:
IBC YYYY (Imperial Basic Charter) • IEA YYYY:NN (Imperial Act) • IED YYYY:NN (Imperial Decree)
MIR YYYY:NN (Ministerial Regulation) • PROV YYYY:NN (Provincial Ordinance)

Rights, Public Order, and Due Process

Rights & Protections

Rights are set out in the Imperial Basic Charter and interpreted by the courts. Limitations must be grounded in law, pursue a legitimate aim (public safety, order, rights of others), and be proportionate.

IBC 1921 — Principles (consolidated excerpt)
• equality before the law • due process • fair trial • lawful property protections
Courts & Enforcement

The Empire maintains a tiered court system (local, regional, supreme) and a professional constabulary. Criminal penalties require lawful judgment; administrative measures require legal basis and review procedures.

Related instruments: IEA 1932:04, IEA 1930:02 (Court Organization Act — index only).

Resources, Trade, and Infrastructure

Environment & Resource Management

Extraction and land use may require permitting and remediation. Imperial standards set minimum protections; provinces may adopt stricter rules if consistent with imperial law.

Related instruments: MIR 2024:14 (Mining Permits), MIR 2023:07 (Water Stewardship).

Trade & Commerce

International trade is governed by customs law, licensing, and tariff schedules. Banking and currency matters fall under the Imperial Treasury and licensed institutions.

Related instruments: IEA 1933:09, MIR 2025:22.

Amendments, Repeals, and Entry into Force

How laws change

  • Acts are enacted by Imperial Parliament and promulgated via the Imperial Gazette.
  • Decrees and regulations must cite enabling authority and are published in the Gazette.
  • Constitutional amendments require a supermajority and imperial ratification.

Entry into force: Unless otherwise specified, instruments enter into force upon Gazette publication.
